# Information Security Policy

**Company:** NimbusCore Inc. (Fictional)  
**Document Owner:** Dana Cho, IT/Security Lead  
**Approved By:** Jordan Mills, CEO  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Review Date:** January 1, 2026  

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NimbusCore Inc.'s information assets and those of its clients. This policy establishes the minimum standards that all employees, contractors, and third parties must follow when accessing or handling company information systems and data.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All NimbusCore Inc. employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and third-party vendors with access to NimbusCore systems or data
- All NimbusCore information systems, including cloud infrastructure, applications, and endpoints
- All data processed, stored, or transmitted on behalf of NimbusCore or its clients

---

## 3. Information Security Objectives

NimbusCore is committed to:

1. Protecting client data, including PII and financial information, from unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss
2. Complying with applicable laws and regulations, including GDPR, CCPA, and TDPSA
3. Ensuring the availability of the NimbusCore platform to meet client SLA commitments
4. Building a security-aware culture through training and clear expectations
5. Continuously improving the security program based on risk assessments and incident findings

---

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| CEO | Executive sponsor; approves security policy; owns reputational and regulatory risk |
| IT/Security Lead | Day-to-day security operations; owns the GRC program; conducts risk assessments |
| CTO | Owns infrastructure and engineering security decisions |
| Head of Engineering | Enforces secure development practices; manages access controls |
| All Employees | Comply with this policy; report security incidents promptly |

---

## 5. Data Classification

All data handled by NimbusCore must be classified into one of the following categories:

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidential** | Highly sensitive data; restricted access; breach notification required | SSNs, bank account numbers, passwords, API keys, client PII |
| **Internal** | Business data not intended for public release | Internal reports, employee records, contracts |
| **Public** | Information approved for public disclosure | Marketing materials, product documentation, job postings |

All employees are responsible for handling data according to its classification level.

---

## 6. Access Control

- Access to NimbusCore systems is granted on a least-privilege basis
- All users must authenticate with a unique username and password
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all accounts
- Access permissions are reviewed quarterly by the IT/Security Lead
- Accounts must be disabled immediately upon employee termination or role change
- Shared or generic accounts are prohibited

---

## 7. Acceptable Use

Employees are permitted to use NimbusCore systems for legitimate business purposes. The following activities are prohibited:

- Accessing, copying, or transmitting client data outside of authorized workflows
- Installing unauthorized software on company-managed devices
- Sharing credentials with any other person
- Storing Confidential data on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services
- Attempting to bypass security controls or access systems without authorization
- Using company resources for illegal or unethical activities

---

## 8. Data Protection

- All Confidential data must be encrypted at rest and in transit
- Client data must not be stored on personal devices
- Data must be retained only for as long as required by applicable regulations or contractual obligations
- Data destruction must follow secure procedures when retention periods expire

---

## 9. Incident Reporting

All employees must immediately report any suspected or confirmed security incident to Dana Cho (IT/Security Lead) at security@nimbuscore.io. This includes:

- Phishing emails that were clicked or responded to
- Lost or stolen devices
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Accidental disclosure of Confidential data
- Ransomware or malware infections

Employees will not be penalized for good-faith incident reporting.

---

## 10. Security Awareness Training

All employees must complete security awareness training:

- Within 30 days of joining NimbusCore
- Annually thereafter

Completion is tracked by the IT/Security Lead. Non-completion will be escalated to the employee's manager.

---

## 11. Third-Party and Vendor Security

All vendors with access to NimbusCore systems or client data must:

- Complete a vendor security assessment before being granted access
- Sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) if they process personal data
- Comply with the minimum security standards defined in NimbusCore's vendor assessment questionnaire

---

## 12. Compliance and Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. Violations involving client data may also result in legal liability.

The IT/Security Lead is responsible for monitoring compliance with this policy.

---

## 13. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or following any significant security incident, regulatory change, or major change to NimbusCore's systems or business model.

---

## Acknowledgment

By accessing NimbusCore systems, all users acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Information Security Policy.
