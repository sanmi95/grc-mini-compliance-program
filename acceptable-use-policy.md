## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines the rules governing how NimbusCore Inc. employees, contractors, and authorized third parties may use company-owned or company-managed information systems, devices, networks, and data. The goal is to protect NimbusCore, its clients, and its employees from harm while enabling productive use of technology.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All NimbusCore employees, contractors, and interns
- All devices owned or managed by NimbusCore, including laptops, mobile devices, and servers
- All NimbusCore cloud services and SaaS applications (AWS, Okta, GitHub, Slack, Datadog, etc.)
- Any personal device used to access NimbusCore systems (BYOD)

---

## 3. Acceptable Uses

The following uses of NimbusCore systems are permitted:

- Performing job duties and authorized business activities
- Communicating with colleagues, clients, and vendors for business purposes
- Accessing company data required to complete assigned work
- Using approved applications listed in the NimbusCore software catalog
- Reasonable incidental personal use that does not interfere with work or violate this policy

---

## 4. Prohibited Uses

The following activities are strictly prohibited on NimbusCore systems or networks:

### 4.1 Unauthorized Access

- Attempting to access systems, data, or accounts for which you have not been granted explicit authorization
- Using another person's credentials to access any NimbusCore system
- Attempting to bypass, disable, or circumvent any security control or monitoring tool

### 4.2 Data Misuse

- Copying, transmitting, or storing Confidential data outside of authorized systems
- Sharing client PII, financial data, or proprietary information with unauthorized parties
- Storing Confidential data on personal devices, USB drives, or unauthorized cloud services (e.g., personal Google Drive, Dropbox)

### 4.3 Malicious Activity

- Installing or distributing malware, spyware, ransomware, or any unauthorized software
- Conducting port scans, penetration tests, or network reconnaissance without prior written authorization
- Launching or participating in denial-of-service attacks
- Engaging in any activity that disrupts the availability of NimbusCore systems or services

### 4.4 Inappropriate Content

- Accessing, downloading, or distributing content that is offensive, discriminatory, harassing, or illegal
- Visiting websites known to distribute malware or pirated content
- Streaming non-work-related media in a way that consumes excessive bandwidth

### 4.5 Legal and Regulatory Violations

- Using NimbusCore systems to violate any applicable law or regulation
- Engaging in copyright infringement, including unauthorized software use
- Conducting personal business activities using NimbusCore resources in a way that creates legal liability

---

## 5. Device Security

All devices used to access NimbusCore systems must:

- Have full-disk encryption enabled
- Have the latest operating system patches applied within 30 days of release
- Run endpoint protection software approved by the IT/Security Lead
- Be locked automatically after 5 minutes of inactivity
- Be reported immediately to IT if lost or stolen

Personal devices (BYOD) used for NimbusCore work must meet the same minimum security requirements. Employees using BYOD do so at their own risk and consent to remote wipe of company data if the device is lost or compromised.

---

## 6. Password Requirements

All passwords for NimbusCore systems must:

- Be at least 14 characters long
- Be unique and not reused across systems
- Be stored in an approved password manager (not written down or stored in plain text)
- Never be shared with anyone, including IT staff

NimbusCore IT will never ask for your password via email, chat, or phone.

---

## 7. Remote Work

Employees working remotely must:

- Connect to NimbusCore systems via the company VPN or approved SSO
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi without a VPN connection
- Ensure their home network is secured with WPA2 or WPA3 encryption
- Prevent unauthorized individuals from viewing their screens when handling Confidential data

---

## 8. Software and Application Use

Only software approved by the IT/Security Lead may be installed on company-managed devices. Employees who require a tool not currently approved must submit a request through the IT request process. Unapproved software discovered on company devices may be removed without notice.

---

## 9. Monitoring

NimbusCore reserves the right to monitor the use of its systems, networks, and devices to ensure compliance with this policy and to protect company and client data. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using NimbusCore systems for any purpose.

Monitoring activities may include network traffic analysis, endpoint logging, email filtering, and cloud activity logging.

---

## 10. Reporting Violations

Employees who observe or suspect a violation of this policy should report it to the IT/Security Lead (Dana Cho) at security@nimbuscore.io. Reports can be made anonymously. Employees will not be retaliated against for good-faith reporting.

---

## 11. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

- A formal warning
- Revocation of system access
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Legal action in cases involving fraud, data theft, or other criminal activity

---

## 12. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by the IT/Security Lead and updated as necessary to reflect changes in technology, regulations, or business operations.
